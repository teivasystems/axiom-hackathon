## Design System

| Parameter | Decision | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Style | **Cinematic** | High-production, enterprise-grade look — matches hackathon judge audience |
| Orientation | **Portrait** | Standard HeyGen pitch video format |
| Pose | **Upper Body** | Shows personality + dress code; avoids full-body awkwardness in video |
| Background | **Dark neutral** (each avatar) | Consistent visual world; focus on face and expression |
| Lighting | **Dramatic studio** (cinematic default) | High contrast, professional |

All seven read as the same team. Each is visually distinct by gender, ethnicity, age, and expression — not by costume gimmicks.

## Ensemble Notes

The seven avatars should feel like they could appear together in a single team photo. Specifically:

- **Consistent background treatment:** all dark, all neutral — no bright colours behind any avatar
- **Consistent lighting register:** all dramatic studio, all cinematic — no avatar should look like a selfie or a stock photo
- **Colour variation in wardrobe only:** charcoal, grey, navy, teal, white — no bright primaries
- **Expression contrast is the differentiator:** Alex (decisive) → Sam (processing) → Morgan (curious) → Jordan (intense) → Casey (precise) → Riley (confident) → Kostya (wry). The emotion arc is intentional.

**Kostya is the punchline visually too.** The other six look like they have a mission. Kostya looks like he has a perspective on the mission. That is the joke, and it should land in the frame before he opens his mouth.
